**Interest Rate Swap Contract**

**General Terms**

This interest rate swap contract is entered into between Party A (Party1) and Party B (Party2) on December 12, 1994. The contract is for a fixed-for-floating interest rate swap, where Party A pays a fixed rate and Party B pays a floating rate based on EUR-LIBOR-BBA.

**Trade Details**

* Trade Identifier: SW2000
* Trade Date: December 12, 1994
* Effective Date: December 14, 1994
* Termination Date: December 14, 1999
* Notional Amount: EUR 50,000,000
* Business Day Convention: Modified Following
* Calculation Period Frequency: 6 months
* Payment Frequency: 6 months
* Reset Frequency: 6 months
* Reset Dates: 2 business days after the last calculation period date
* Payment Dates: 2 business days after the last calculation period date

**Fixed Rate Terms**

* Fixed Rate: 4.427% per annum
* Fixed Rate Day Count: ACT/360
* Fixed Rate Calculation Period: From and including the effective date to but excluding the termination date

**Floating Rate Terms**

* Floating Rate: EUR-LIBOR-BBA
* Floating Rate Day Count: 30E/360
* Floating Rate Calculation Period: From and including the effective date to but excluding the termination date
* Floating Rate Reset Dates: 2 business days after the last calculation period date
* Floating Rate Payment Dates: 2 business days after the last calculation period date

**Counterparty Details**

* Party A: Party1
* Party B: Party2
* Counterparty Roles: Party A is the fixed rate payer, and Party B is the floating rate payer

**Transfer History**

* Transfer History: There is one transfer history entry, where Party A pays EUR 2,500.40 to Party B on December 14, 1994.

**Representations**

* Each party represents that they are acting for their own account and have made their own independent decisions to enter into this contract.
* Each party represents that they are capable of assessing the merits and risks of this contract and have sufficient financial resources to bear the risks associated with this contract.

**Risk Disclosure**

* There are significant risks associated with this contract, including interest rate risk, credit risk, and liquidity risk.
* Party B should consult their own financial, legal, accounting, and tax advisors about the risks associated with this contract.

**Ordinary Course of Business**

* In the ordinary course of their business, Party A or its affiliates may affect transactions for their own account or for the account of their customers.
* Party A is not obliged to take into account the circumstances of the parties to this contract or act in a manner that is favorable to them.

**Underlying Exposure**

* This contract has been entered into to hedge the risk arising out of the underlying exposure, which is a USD 10,000,000 fixed rate loan maturing on or after October 5, 2033.

**Purpose of the Transaction**

* The purpose of this interest rate swap transaction is to convert the fixed rate USD liability of Party B into a floating rate liability with the view that future interest rates will be lower than currently projected.
