**Interest Rate Swap Contract**

This contract is an interest rate swap between two parties, Party A and Party B. The contract is dated February 20, 2005, and is effective until February 22, 2035.

**General Terms**

*   **Notional Amount**: The contract has two notional amounts, £100,000 and £100,000, for each of the two legs of the swap.
*   **Interest Rate**: Party A pays a fixed interest rate of 4.427% per annum, while Party B pays a floating interest rate based on the GBP-LIBOR-3M rate.
*   **Calculation Period**: The calculation period for both legs of the swap is from February 22, 2005, to February 22, 2035.
*   **Payment Dates**: Party A pays annually on October 7, and Party B pays annually on October 7, with the first payment date being October 7, 2005.
*   **Day Count**: The day count convention for both legs of the swap is Actual/360.

**Swap Terms**

*   **Swap Type**: The swap is a fixed-for-floating interest rate swap.
*   **Fixed Rate**: Party A pays a fixed interest rate of 4.427% per annum.
*   **Floating Rate**: Party B pays a floating interest rate based on the GBP-LIBOR-3M rate.
*   **Reset Dates**: The reset dates for the floating rate are every 3 months, starting from February 22, 2005.
*   **Compounding Method**: The compounding method for the floating rate is flat.

**Counterparty Information**

*   **Party A**: Party A is identified as "Party A" with a party ID of "12345".
*   **Party B**: Party B is identified as "Party B" with a party ID of "67890".

**Account Information**

*   **Account Number**: The account number for Party A is "PRIM_450", and the account number for Party B is "CROSS_560".
*   **Account Name**: The account name for Party A is "PRIMARY_ACCOUNT", and the account name for Party B is "CROSS_ACCOUNT".

**Risk Disclosure**

*   **Interest Rate Risk**: The market value of the swap may be adversely affected if interest rates move from their current positions.
*   **Fixing Risk**: The NET payments made by Party B will increase if the GBP-LIBOR-3M fixing increases.
*   **Liquidity Risk**: The swap is not a readily liquid instrument, and there may be a lack of liquidity or low trading volume in the market for the swap.
*   **Credit Risk**: The swap carries the credit risk of Party A.

**Representations**

*   **Party A Representations**: Party A represents that it is acting for its own account and has made its own independent decisions to enter into the swap.
*   **Party B Representations**: Party B represents that it is entering into the swap solely for the purpose of hedging its underlying exposure and not for purposes of speculation.

**Acceptance**

*   **Acceptance**: The parties accept the terms and conditions of the swap and acknowledge that they have read and understood the risk disclosure statement.
