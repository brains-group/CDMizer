**Credit Default Swap Contract**

This contract is a Credit Default Swap (CDS) between Party 1 and Party 2, effective from December 2, 2002, and terminating on December 3, 2007.

**General Terms**

* The notional amount of the contract is USD 20,000,000.
* The contract is based on a single-name credit default swap, with the reference entity being the Kingdom of Thailand.
* The reference obligation is a USD-denominated bond with the ISIN code US88322KAC53.
* The credit event notice is publicly available, and the notifying party is Party 2.
* The credit event notice is triggered by a failure to pay, obligation acceleration, repudiation moratorium, or restructuring.

**Payout Terms**

* The credit default payout is based on the price quantity of USD 1,000,000.
* The settlement currency is USD.
* The settlement type is physical, with a physical settlement period of 30 business days.
* The deliverable obligations are bonds or loans, not subordinate, and not sovereign lenders.
* The maximum maturity of the deliverable obligations is 30 years.

**Calculation Agent**

* The calculation agent is an independent party.
* The calculation agent's business center is GBLO.

**Trade Lot**

* The trade lot consists of a single price quantity of USD 20,000,000.

**Counterparty**

* Party 1 is the buyer of the credit default swap.
* Party 2 is the seller of the credit default swap.

**Ancillary Party**

* The calculation agent is an ancillary party to the contract.

**Documentation**

* The contract is documented under the ISDA Master Agreement.
* The contractual definitions are based on the ISDA 1999 Credit Derivatives Definitions.

**Transfer History**

* There is a single transfer history entry, dated January 2, 2023.
* The transfer is a payment of USD 1,200 from Party 2 to Party 1.
* The transfer is an upfront payment.

**Representations**

* Each party represents that it has the power to enter into the contract and perform its obligations.
* Each party represents that it is not relying on any communication from the other party as investment advice.
* Each party represents that it is capable of assessing the merits and risks of the contract.

**Risk Disclosure**

* The contract carries significant risks, including credit risk, liquidity risk, and market risk.
* The parties should consult their own financial, legal, accounting, and tax advisors before entering into the contract.

**Ordinary Course of Business**

* JPMorgan or its affiliates may affect transactions for their own account or for the account of their customers.
* Neither JPMorgan nor its affiliates is obliged to take into account the circumstances of the parties to the contract.

**Potential Conflicts of Interest**

* Potential conflicts of interest may exist in the structure and operation of the strategy and the course of the normal business activities of JPMorgan or its affiliates.

**Underlying Exposure**

* The contract is entered into to hedge the risk arising out of the underlying exposure of USD 10,000,000 fixed-rate loan, maturing on or after December 3, 2007.

**Purpose of the Transaction**

* The purpose of the credit default swap is to convert the fixed-rate liability of Party 2 into a floating-rate liability.

**Scenario Analysis**

* The scenario analysis assumes various levels of USD SOFR-Compound, which represents low, medium, and high-risk scenarios.
* The resultant cash flow gain/loss to Party 2 is calculated based on the scenario analysis.

**Mark to Market (MTM) Sensitivity Analysis**

* The MTM sensitivity analysis is provided for illustrative purposes only.
* The analysis assumes parallel shifts in the USD SOFR-Compound curve.
