**Contract Description**

This is a Credit Default Swap (CDS) contract between Party 1 and Party 2. The contract is dated December 1, 2006, and has a termination date of March 21, 2010.

**General Terms**

* The contract is a single-name CDS, referencing Company DEF.
* The notional amount is USD 10,257,000.
* The protection buyer (Party 1) pays a premium to the protection seller (Party 2) based on the credit spread of Company DEF.
* The credit spread is calculated using the SOFR-Compound rate.

**Payment Terms**

* The protection buyer (Party 1) pays the premium to the protection seller (Party 2) on a quarterly basis.
* The premium is calculated using the SOFR-Compound rate and the notional amount.
* The protection seller (Party 2) pays the protection buyer (Party 1) in the event of a credit event, such as bankruptcy or failure to pay.

**Credit Event Terms**

* The credit event is triggered if Company DEF experiences a bankruptcy or failure to pay.
* The protection seller (Party 2) must notify the protection buyer (Party 1) of the credit event within a specified timeframe.
* The protection buyer (Party 1) has the option to accelerate the payment of the premium to the protection seller (Party 2) in the event of a credit event.

**Calculation Agent**

* The calculation agent is Calculation Agent Independent.
* The calculation agent is responsible for calculating the premium and the credit event payment.

**Governing Law**

* The contract is governed by English law.

**Representations**

* Each party represents that it has the power to enter into the contract and that it is not relying on any communication from the other party as investment advice.

**Risk Disclosure**

* The contract carries significant risks, including credit risk, liquidity risk, and market risk.
* The protection buyer (Party 1) may lose its entire investment or incur an unlimited loss in the event of a credit event.
