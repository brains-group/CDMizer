**Credit Default Swap Contract**

**General Terms**

This Credit Default Swap (CDS) contract is entered into between Party 1 (the buyer) and Party 2 (the seller) on June 16, 2009. The contract is for a single-name credit default swap with a notional amount of USD 20,000,000.

**Trade Details**

* Trade Date: June 8, 2009
* Effective Date: June 16, 2009
* Termination Date: June 20, 2012
* Mandatory Early Termination Date: Not applicable
* Business Day Convention: Modified Following
* Calculation Agent: Party 2 (unless stated otherwise in ISDA)

**Counterparty Information**

* Party 1: The buyer is identified as "Party 1" with a global reference of "7156036b" and an external reference of "_broker2".
* Party 2: The seller is identified as "Party 2" with a global reference of "a2b7933d" and an external reference of "_fund".

**Trade Lot**

* The trade lot consists of a single credit default swap with a notional amount of USD 20,000,000.
* The swap has a fixed rate of 4.427% per annum, paid by Party 1 to Party 2.
* The swap has a floating rate of USD SOFR-COMPOUND, paid by Party 2 to Party 1.
* The calculation period for both parties is from and including October 5 to, but excluding, October 5 of the consecutive year.
* The payment dates for both parties are annually 2 business days after October 5 of each year, commencing on October 7, 2009, and ending on October 7, 2013.

**Mandatory Early Termination Clause**

This swap shall be terminated on the Mandatory Early Termination Date at the market value of the swap, as on the Mandatory Early Termination Date, subject to adjustment in accordance with the Modified Following Business Day Convention.

**Risk Disclosure**

There are significant risks associated with this Transaction, including, but not limited to, foreign exchange risk, price risk, liquidity risk, and credit risk. Counterparties should consult their own financial, legal, accounting, and tax advisors about the risks associated with this Transaction.

**Representations**

Each party represents to the other party that it is acting for its own account, has made its own independent decisions to enter into this Transaction, and is not relying on any communication of the other party as investment advice or as a recommendation to enter into this Transaction.

**Underlying Exposure**

The purpose of this credit default swap is to hedge the risk arising out of the underlying exposure of USD 10,000,000 fixed-rate loan, maturing on or after October 5, 2033.
