**Credit Default Swap Index Contract**

This contract is a Credit Default Swap Index (CDSI) contract between two parties, Bank X and Bank Y, with Up&Atem and ClearItAll as clearing organizations. The contract is dated April 1, 2018, and is subject to the terms of the ISDA Master Agreement.

**General Terms**

* Trade Date: April 1, 2018
* Effective Date: April 1, 2018
* Termination Date: March 20, 2029
* Notional Amount: USD 10,000,000
* Business Day Convention: Modified Following
* Fixing Business Days: US Government Bonds Market
* Business Days: New York
* Calculation Agent: Bank X (unless stated otherwise in ISDA)
* Governing Law: English
* Documentation: ISDA

**Party A Payments**

* Bank X pays (in USD): 4.427% p.a. on the USD Notional Amount
* Calculation Period: From and including the Effective Date to, but excluding, the Termination Date
* Payment Dates: Annually 2 Business Days after the Effective Date, commencing on October 7, 2024, up to and including October 7, 2029

**Party B Payments**

* Bank Y pays (in USD): USD SOFR-COMPOUND p.a. on the USD Notional Amount
* Calculation Period: From and including the Effective Date to, but excluding, the Termination Date
* Payment Dates: Annually 2 Business Days after the Effective Date, commencing on October 7, 2024, up to and including October 7, 2029

**Mandatory Early Termination Clause**

* The contract shall be terminated on the Mandatory Early Termination Date at the market value of the contract, as on the Mandatory Early Termination Date, subject to adjustment in accordance with the Modified Following Business Day Convention.
* On 2 Business Days after the Mandatory Early Termination Date, the party which is out-of-the-money shall pay to the party which is in-the-money, the Cash Settlement Amount, which shall be an amount in USD equal to the absolute value of the market value of the contract, as determined on the Mandatory Early Termination Date.

**Risk Disclosure**

* There are significant risks associated with this contract, including, but not limited to, foreign exchange risk, price risk, liquidity risk, and credit risk.
* Counterparties should consult their own financial, legal, accounting, and tax advisors about the risks associated with this contract, the appropriate tools to analyze the contract, and the suitability of the contract in each counterparty's particular circumstances.

**Interest Rate Risk**

* If USD interest rates move from their current positions, the market value of the contract may be adversely affected from Bank Y's perspective.

**Fixing Risk**

* If USD SOFR-COMPOUND fixings increase, the NET payments made by Bank Y will increase.

**Liquidity Risk**

* This contract is not a readily liquid instrument. There may exist a time when there is a lack of liquidity or low trading volume in the market for the contract, and this potential illiquidity could significantly decrease the market value of the contract for Bank Y.

**Credit Risk**

* The contract carries the credit risk of Bank X.

In conducting such business, neither Bank X nor any of its affiliates or subsidiaries is obliged to take into account the circumstances of the parties to the contract or act in a manner which is favorable to them.

**Potential Conflicts of Interest**

* Potential conflicts of interest may exist in the structure and operation of the strategy and the course of the normal business activities of Bank X or any of its affiliates or subsidiaries or their respective directors, officers, employees, representatives, delegates, or agents of their normal business activities.

**Underlying Exposure**

* This contract has been entered to hedge the risk arising out of the underlying exposure, which is a USD 10,000,000 fixed rate loan, maturing on or after October 5, 2033.

**Purpose of the Contract**

* The purpose of the credit default swap index contract is to convert the fixed rate USD liability of Bank Y into a floating rate liability with the view that future interest rates will be lower than currently projected.
