**Credit Default Swap Contract**

This contract is a Credit Default Swap (CDS) between Party 1 and Party 2, effective from December 2, 2002, and terminating on December 3, 2007.

**General Terms**

* The notional amount of the contract is USD 20,000,000.
* The contract is based on a single-name credit default swap, with the reference entity being the Kingdom of Thailand.
* The reference obligation is a USD-denominated bond with the ISIN code US88322KAC53.
* The credit event trigger is failure to pay, with a payment requirement of USD 10,000,000.
* The protection buyer (Party 2) pays a premium of USD 4.427% per annum on the notional amount.
* The protection seller (Party 1) pays the difference between the reference price and the market price of the reference obligation in the event of a credit event.

**Calculation Agent**

* The calculation agent is Calculation Agent Independent.
* The calculation agent's business center is GBLO.

**Termination Date**

* The contract terminates on December 3, 2007, unless terminated earlier by a mandatory early termination clause.

**Mandatory Early Termination Clause**

* The contract may be terminated on November 3, 2007, at the market value of the contract, subject to adjustment in accordance with the modified following business day convention.

**Payout**

* In the event of a credit event, the protection buyer (Party 2) receives the difference between the reference price and the market price of the reference obligation, minus the payment requirement.
* The payout is made in USD and is subject to the physical settlement terms.

**Physical Settlement Terms**

* The physical settlement period is 30 business days.
* The deliverable obligations are bonds or loans that meet certain criteria, including being non-subordinated, not sovereign, and not domestic law.
* The specified currency is USD.
* The maximum maturity of the deliverable obligations is 30 years.

**Escrow**

* The contract includes an escrow clause, which requires the protection seller (Party 1) to hold the deliverable obligations in escrow until the physical settlement period has expired.

**Transfer History**

* There is one transfer history entry, which shows a transfer of USD 1,200 from Party 2 to Party 1 on January 2, 2023.

**Representations**

* Each party represents that it has the power to enter into the contract and has taken all necessary action to authorize its execution and performance.
* Each party represents that its entry into and performance of the contract does not result in a breach or violation of any applicable law or regulation.

**Risk Disclosure**

* The contract carries significant risks, including credit risk, liquidity risk, and market risk.
* The protection buyer (Party 2) should consult its own financial, legal, accounting, and tax advisors about the risks associated with the contract.

**Ordinary Course of Business**

* In the ordinary course of their business, the parties may affect transactions for their own account or for the account of their customers.
* Neither party is obliged to take into account the circumstances of the other party or act in a manner that is favorable to the other party.

**Potential Conflicts of Interest**

* Potential conflicts of interest may exist in the structure and operation of the contract and the course of the normal business activities of the parties.

**Underlying Exposure**

* The contract has been entered into to hedge the risk arising out of the underlying exposure of Party 2, which is a USD 10,000,000 fixed-rate loan maturing on or after December 3, 2007.

**Purpose of the Transaction**

* The purpose of the contract is to convert the fixed-rate liability of Party 2 into a floating-rate liability with the view that future interest rates will be lower than currently projected.

**Scenario Analysis**

* The contract includes a scenario analysis, which shows the potential cash flow gain or loss to Party 2 under different scenarios, including a scenario where USD SOFR-COMPOUND remains unchanged, increases, or decreases over the tenor of the contract.

**Mark to Market (MTM) Sensitivity Analysis**

* The contract includes an MTM sensitivity analysis, which shows the potential change in the mark-to-market value of the contract due to parallel shifts in the USD SOFR-COMPOUND curve.
